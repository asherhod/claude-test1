# Mangalist — Strategy

## What this is
Online store for gas grills ("מנגל גז") and BBQ accessories in Israel. Domain: mangalist.co.il.

- Twins (21, post-army) — day-to-day operations: receive orders, handle deliveries, close deals with importers/suppliers for different grill types and accessories.
- **Nadav** (one of the twins) — joined 2026-07-05 as a **manager member** alongside the parent and the virtual CEO: can direct Galisto and the subagents directly for day-to-day work. No new approval authority — the parent remains the sole sign-off on financial/execution decisions.
- Parent — funds the business, sole decision-maker for execution on everything, not just financial matters.
- Virtual CEO (Claude Code) — directs the four subagents below rather than doing their work itself; lets them consult each other on cross-domain questions; only once satisfied with the resulting plan brings it to the parent. See `../CLAUDE.md` for the full Operating process and Approval Rules.
- **CFO subagent** (`.claude/agents/cfo.md`) — financial reports, revenue, expenses, budget tracking (`finance.md`).
- **COO subagent** (`.claude/agents/coo.md`) — all operational matters: purchase/supplier tracking, delivery confirmation, customer satisfaction with received products (`operations.md`), plus product/technical grill evaluation to support the twins' supplier negotiations. Supports the twins, doesn't replace them.
- **CIO subagent** (`.claude/agents/cio.md`) — all technology matters: the website, stack/architecture, information security, code quality, hosting.
- **Marketing & Sales subagent** (`.claude/agents/marketing.md`) — brand voice/positioning, site copy, all distribution/marketing channels, and sales targets (`marketing.md`).
- All four report to the virtual CEO; the same financial approval rule applies to anything that costs money, and nothing any of them produces is final/live until the parent approves it.

## Decided: product scope / brands
**Locked 2026-07-04 by parent.** Launch with two brands, spanning two price tiers:
- **Char-Broil** — entry/mid tier, good margins, TRU-Infrared flare-up-prevention as a selling point.
- **Napoleon** — premium tier, matches parent's existing brand trust.

Weber and Broil King are deprioritized for launch (Weber: oversaturated Israeli market, hard to differentiate on price; Broil King: overlaps Napoleon's tier without adding anything). Revisit adding brands once Char-Broil/Napoleon are established. See `suppliers.md` for the importer leads to pursue. Since Mangalist will be one of many resellers of these same brands, differentiation needs to come from service/price/niche (e.g. balcony/portable grills for Israeli apartments), not brand exclusivity.

## Open decisions (need answers before the next phase)
- Supplier sourcing: contact Explore (Char-Broil) and the Napoleon distributor leads in `suppliers.md` to find wholesale/reseller terms — which importer(s) the twins approach first, and what terms (pricing, exclusivity, minimum order quantities) are acceptable.

- Payment gateway — **CFO/CIO recommendation, pending parent approval (2026-07-04):** lean toward **Tranzila** — clearing fees ~0.9–1.3%, terminal can be opened within ~24 hours with no financial guarantees required, which fits a brand-new business without a trading history. **Cardcom** (~1.2% fees, API-first, popular with developers) is a reasonable alternative if deeper custom checkout integration is needed later — not needed yet given the site has no real checkout. Before signing anything: confirm actual quoted fee (rates vary by risk profile), commitment period, and Hebrew support directly with the provider. No contract signed — nothing here is decided.

- Shipping/courier — **COO recommendation, pending parent approval (2026-07-04):** gas grills are bulky/heavy — standard small-parcel couriers likely won't fit well; accessories (small items) are a different shipping profile than grills themselves. Recommend getting direct freight/large-item quotes from 2–3 providers (e.g. HFD, OneStop — both offer logistics/warehousing suited to bulkier goods) rather than committing to one now, since actual per-shipment cost depends heavily on volume/weight and there's no order history yet to size that against.

- Fulfillment model — **CFO recommendation, pending parent approval (2026-07-04):** lean toward **order-on-demand from suppliers rather than holding inventory** at launch. Gas grills are expensive and bulky per unit — holding stock would tie up a large share of the 30,000–50,000 NIS budget and require storage space, with no sales data yet to justify which SKUs would actually move. Revisit once there's a few months of real order data to see which models justify holding stock.

- **Phase 1 site architecture — CIO proposal, pending CEO/parent approval (2026-07-04):** parent approved moving the site from a static HTML/CSS/JS shell to a real database-backed marketing/sales site (product catalog + structured lead capture; checkout/payment/shipping still deferred). Original recommendation: Supabase-managed Postgres (data + auto-generated API/access-control layer) behind the existing static frontend (kept as plain HTML/CSS/JS, no framework/build step), hosted free on Netlify or Cloudflare Pages; twins self-serve catalog edits and view leads via Supabase's built-in Studio dashboard (spreadsheet-like table editor) rather than a custom-built CMS.
  - **Revised 2026-07-04 per parent direction:** parent wants everything built and run locally on their own Windows machine first, reviewed together, and only then deployed — with **AWS** as the eventual deploy target (not Netlify/Cloudflare/hosted Supabase). CIO evaluated two local-build paths and recommends **self-hosted Supabase via Docker** (full stack — Postgres + PostgREST + RLS + Studio — run locally via Docker Desktop, same docker-compose later deployed to a single AWS EC2 instance) over a fully custom AWS-native stack (hand-built Node/Express API + Postgres + hand-built admin panel, targeting RDS/EC2/S3/CloudFront). Reasoning: the self-hosted-Supabase path gives the twins a ready, tested table-editor UI (Studio) on day one instead of requiring the CIO to hand-build an admin panel from scratch, and the local-to-EC2 path is a near-unchanged lift (same docker-compose, swap secrets/TLS/security groups) rather than a rewrite. Flagged honestly: fully AWS-native (Option 2) is judged **scope creep** relative to the business's actual current scale (2-person team, no orders yet, small catalog) — its main edge is a somewhat lower steady-state AWS bill, but at the cost of substantially more custom code (auth, access control, and the admin UI itself) that only the CIO would be building and maintaining solo. Real downside of the recommended path acknowledged: self-hosted Supabase's container patching/CVE hygiene and Postgres backups become the CIO's ongoing responsibility (no managed-service safety net), to be built into the plan (scheduled pg_dump-to-S3 backups, periodic image updates) rather than treated as free. Estimated AWS run-rate once deployed: roughly 60–150 NIS/month (single EC2 instance sized for the full stack, e.g. t3.small/medium, no managed RDS/ALB), inside the CFO's approved 150–450 NIS/month band. Nothing installed, provisioned, or paid for yet — full comparison given to the CEO; awaiting CEO sign-off then parent approval before any build work starts.

Replaced the original narrower "CTO" role (2026-07-04) per parent's direction. **Restructured 2026-07-04** per parent's explicit direction: CIO manages direct reports (has the `Agent` tool to spawn/direct them itself) rather than implementing everything by hand — CIO sets architecture/direction, reviews consolidated output, and reports up to the CEO only once QA has signed off. **Expanded 2026-07-05** per parent's direction to add Solution Architect and DBA as two further direct reports, splitting design and data-layer ownership out from Dev.
  - **Solution Architect** (`.claude/agents/solution-architect.md`) — owns detailed technical design/architecture for significant new builds (new integrations, deployment topology, major schema/system redesign) before Dev implements; evaluates technology/vendor tradeoffs and recommends, doesn't decide business direction.
  - **DBA** (`.claude/agents/dba.md`) — owns the data layer: Postgres schema/migrations design, indexing, RLS/access-control policy correctness, backup/restore strategy for the self-hosted Supabase stack. Reviews schema/migration changes before they go to QA.
  - **Dev** (`.claude/agents/dev.md`) — hands-on implementation (code, schema, integrations). Takes direction from CIO (and the design/schema from Solution Architect/DBA where applicable), not the CEO directly; flags architecture decisions back to CIO rather than deciding them itself.
  - **QA** (`.claude/agents/qa.md`) — reports to CIO, not the CEO directly. Independently writes and executes test scenarios against anything Dev builds; logs results in `qa.md`. A build is only reported up the chain (CIO → CEO → parent) as done once QA has signed off with no known bugs — QA doesn't fix bugs or make architecture calls itself, it verifies and reports back to CIO, who routes fixes to Dev.
- **Marketing & Sales** (`.claude/agents/marketing.md`) — brand/copy/channels/sales targets only.

Staff consult each other directly on cross-domain questions (e.g. COO asks CFO about purchase budget, Marketing asks CIO about site feasibility) rather than routing everything through the CEO.
